# All Account Types Sure Offers

Sure supports a comprehensive range of account types to track your complete financial picture. This page provides a complete reference of all available account types and their subtypes.

## Overview: Assets vs. Liabilities

| Classification | Types | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Assets** | Cash, Investment, Crypto, Property, Vehicle, Other Asset | Things you own |
| **Liabilities** | Credit Card, Loan, Other Liability | Things you owe |

### Loan

Track mortgages, auto loans, student loans, and other installment debt.

| Subtype | Short Name | Full Name |
| --- | --- | --- |
| mortgage | Mortgage | Mortgage |
| student | Student | Student Loan |
| auto | Auto | Auto Loan |
| other | Other | Other Loan |

**Additional fields:**

| Field | Description |
| --- | --- |

| Rate Type | Fixed or variable |
| Term (months) | Loan duration |

> For fixed-rate loans, Sure automatically calculates the monthly payment based on your original balance, interest rate, and term.

### Other Liability

Debts that don't fit the above categories:
- Personal loans from friends/family
- Medical bills
- IOUs
- Tax obligations

## Tax Treatment Explained

Investment accounts have different tax implications:

| Treatment | Description | When Taxes Apply |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Taxable** | Normal taxation | Gains taxed when realized |
| **Tax-Deferred** | Taxes postponed | Taxes paid on withdrawal |
| **Tax-Exempt** | No taxes on gains | Qualified withdrawals are tax-free |
| **Tax-Advantaged** | Special benefits | Varies by account type and use |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Tax treatment in Sure is for informational and organizational purposes. Consult a tax professional for tax advice specific to your situation.

## Account Sharing and Permissions

Sure supports sharing accounts with other family members, allowing collaborative financial management while maintaining appropriate access controls.

### How Account Sharing Works

Accounts can be shared with other members of your family with different permission levels:

**Permission Levels:**

| Permission | Can View | Can Edit | Can Delete |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **View Only** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No | ❌ No |
| **Edit** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| **Full Access** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |

### Sharing an Account

To share an account with family members:

1. Navigate to the account you want to share
2. Open the account settings or details page
3. Find the "Sharing" or "Permissions" section
4. Click **Share Account** or **Manage Access**
5. Select the family member you want to share with
6. Choose their permission level
7. Click **Save** or **Share**

### Managing Shared Accounts

**As an account owner:**
- You maintain full control over accounts you create
- You can modify or revoke sharing permissions at any time
- You can see who has access to each account

**As a shared user:**
- Shared accounts appear in your account list
- Your available actions depend on your permission level
- You can see who owns the account

### Account Scoping

Account sharing affects how data appears throughout Sure:

**Dashboard and reports:**
- Your dashboard shows all accounts you own plus accounts shared with you
- Net worth calculations include all accessible accounts
- Reports and budgets can be filtered by account ownership

**Transactions and activity:**
- You can view transactions for any account you have access to
- Edit permissions allow you to categorize, tag, and modify transactions
- Full access allows you to delete transactions and manage account settings

**Provider connections:**
- Only account owners and users with full access can manage provider connections
- Synced data is visible to all users with access to the account

### Family-Level Features

Some features operate at the family level and are accessible to all family members:

- **Recurring transactions**: Scoped to accounts you have access to
- **Merchants**: Shared across the family for consistent categorization
- **Categories and tags**: Available to all family members
- **Rules**: Can be created by any family member and apply to their accessible accounts

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Account sharing is designed for trusted family members. Users with edit or full access permissions can modify financial data, so only share accounts with people you trust.

### Privacy and Security

**What's shared:**
- Account balances and transaction history
- Holdings and investment details (for investment accounts)
- Account metadata (name, type, institution)

**What's not shared:**
- Your login credentials
- Provider API keys (unless explicitly configured)
- Personal settings and preferences

**Admin controls:**
- Family admins can manage provider connections for the entire family
- Only admins can configure third-party provider integrations
- Account owners always retain control over their own accounts
